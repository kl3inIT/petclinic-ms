# PetClinic Knowledge Base

## Vaccination Schedule

### Dogs (Puppy & Adult)
- 6-8 weeks: First DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)
- 10-12 weeks: Second DHPP, Bordetella (kennel cough)
- 14-16 weeks: Third DHPP, Rabies, Leptospirosis
- 12-16 months: DHPP booster, Rabies booster
- Annually thereafter: Rabies + DHPP every 1-3 years, Bordetella every 6-12 months for socialised dogs.

### Cats (Kitten & Adult)
- 6-8 weeks: First FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia)
- 10-12 weeks: Second FVRCP, FeLV (Feline Leukemia) test + vaccine for outdoor cats
- 14-16 weeks: Third FVRCP, Rabies, FeLV booster if applicable
- 12-16 months: FVRCP booster, Rabies booster
- Annually thereafter: Rabies + FVRCP every 1-3 years.

## Common Symptoms — When to Book a Visit

### Routine (book within 1-3 days)
- Mild lethargy lasting > 24h
- Reduced appetite for > 24h with normal water intake
- Mild diarrhea without blood
- Excessive scratching, mild skin redness
- Gradual weight loss
- Dental tartar buildup, bad breath

### Urgent (same-day visit)
- Vomiting more than 3 times in 12h
- Diarrhea with blood
- Limping persisting > 24h
- Eye discharge or squinting
- Difficulty urinating, frequent attempts
- Sudden behavior changes

### Emergency (immediate ER visit, not regular booking)
- Difficulty breathing, blue gums
- Suspected poisoning (chocolate, grapes, xylitol, antifreeze, lily exposure for cats)
- Severe trauma, hit by car, falls from height
- Seizures lasting > 2 minutes or cluster seizures
- Bloated abdomen, especially in large dogs (possible GDV)
- Heatstroke symptoms (panting + drooling + collapse)
- Allergic reaction with facial swelling
- Inability to urinate (especially male cats — urinary blockage is fatal)
- Pale gums, weakness, collapse
- Active uncontrolled bleeding

## Routine Care by Life Stage

### Puppies / Kittens (0-12 months)
- Vet visits: monthly until 16 weeks, then 6-month check.
- Deworming: every 2-4 weeks until 12 weeks, then monthly until 6 months.
- Spay/neuter discussion: typically 5-9 months for dogs, 4-6 months for cats.
- Nutrition: high-calorie growth formula appropriate for breed size.

### Adult (1-7 years for most breeds)
- Annual wellness exam + vaccines.
- Dental cleaning every 1-2 years (varies by breed).
- Heartworm prevention monthly year-round.
- Flea/tick prevention monthly (geography-dependent).

### Senior (7+ years for large dogs, 10+ for small dogs/cats)
- Bi-annual wellness exams.
- Annual blood panel + urinalysis.
- Watch for: lumps, weight changes, drinking/urinating more, stiffness, vision changes.
- Joint supplements may be recommended.

## Common Breed-Specific Notes

### Brachycephalic Breeds (Pugs, Bulldogs, Boxers, Persians)
- Higher risk of breathing difficulty in heat. Avoid exercise above 28°C.
- Risk of brachycephalic obstructive airway syndrome (BOAS) — assess at 6-12 months.
- Anesthesia requires extra monitoring.

### Large + Giant Breeds (Great Dane, German Shepherd, Labrador, etc.)
- Watch for hip/elbow dysplasia from puppy hood — gait check every visit.
- GDV (bloat) risk — feed 2 smaller meals/day, avoid heavy exercise post-meal.
- Cardiac screening (DCM) recommended annually for Dobermans, Boxers, Great Danes.

### Small Breeds (Yorkie, Chihuahua, Pomeranian, etc.)
- Dental disease common from age 2-3 — anesthetic cleaning often needed earlier.
- Hypoglycemia risk in toy breeds < 1.5kg — feed every 4-6h until 6 months.
- Patella luxation common — orthopedic check yearly.

### Cats (general)
- Indoor cats live ~15-18 years; outdoor cats 5-8 years.
- FIV + FeLV testing recommended at first visit.
- Hair-ball control: brushing 2-3x/week + dietary fiber.
- Litter box: 1 per cat + 1 extra. Watch for changes in usage as early kidney/UTI sign.

## Pre-Visit Preparation Checklist

For the owner:
- Bring vaccination records (or share with clinic in advance).
- Note when symptoms started + frequency + triggers.
- For vomiting/diarrhea: photo of stool/vomit helpful (yes really).
- Bring current medications + dosages.
- For diet questions: bring current food label.

For the pet:
- Cats: carrier with familiar blanket. Spray Feliway 15 min before.
- Dogs: leash + comfortable harness. Skip food 2-4h before if motion-prone.
- Senior pets: bring water + favorite blanket for waiting room comfort.

## Insurance + Payment Notes

- Most pet insurance is reimbursement-based — owner pays clinic, claims afterwards.
- Pre-existing conditions usually excluded — enroll early (8-12 weeks ideal).
- Wellness plans cover routine visits + vaccines monthly; separate from accident/illness.
- Emergency visits typically NOT covered by wellness plans alone.
- Most clinics accept cards; some have CareCredit / Scratchpay for financing.
